Milan Van de Berg

Name: Milan Van de Berg

Age: 9

Group: 7

Location: Groningen

School: Openbare Bredero Basisschool at Ina Boudierplantsoen 3, 9721 XA Groningen

Motivations: Having fun and playing, going through the school day and finish his homework fast so he can play games.

Games: Fortnite, Minecraft, Toca Kitchen 2

Technology: Tablet, phone, computer

Biography

Milan is a 9 year old Dutch boy living in the southern part of the city of Groningen. He has a younger sister he likes to play video games with and that sometimes he is asked to help with homework, a task he dislikes. His parents work as an IT specialist and an HR manager in the same company.

Goals

  • He wants to play more often
  • He wants learning to be easier and more interactive
  • He wants to learn but loses focus quickly

Frustrations

  • School is boring because it is mostly book based and reading isn’t fun
  • History is even more boring because who cares about people 200 years ago? They’re like dinosaurs!
  • Sometimes the language used in texts is too difficult to understand

Needs

  • More visual and interactive, and less text based teaching
  • Colourful & attention catching
  • Good visuals

Habits

  • Going to school 5 days a week
  • Studying using books and school tablet
  • Playing games on his phone/tablet
  • Often loses focus/gets distracted easily
  • Stares outside if lesson material bores him
  • Doesn’t like reading so he often skips out on that

Name: Mark Wieringa

Age: 34

Occupation: Teacher of group 7

Location: Groningen

School: Openbare Bredero Basisschool at Ina Boudierplantsoen 3, 9721 XA Groningen

Motivations: Likes to inspire his pupils. Is very strict when it comes to being late. Shows interest in new technology and teaching methods.

Biography

Mark got married 5 years ago, and has an 8 month old kid. He is often tired at work and is generally very stressed. He enjoyed his work but has a feeling the entire system could do with improvements. The way children are taught has barely been changed over the past 30 years, after all.

Goals

  • Increasing the engagement with the classroom
  • Inspiring the children to perform well in school and in their personal endeavors
  • Build a safe environment for his pupils
  • Improve the image of the school
  • Discipline the children

Frustrations

  • Children have very short attention spans when it comes to lesson material
  • Children blame the teacher for not getting good grades, despite them not doing anything
  • Children form groups, leaving a few children out
  • Trying to pay equal attention to everyone is very demanding

Needs

  • A way to see who needs the extra attention
  • A way to make children excited for specific courses (history in this case)
  • A modern way of teaching to better suit the needs of the current generation of pupils

Habits

  • Gives the children incentives for doing their best (allowing them to write with a colored gel pen when getting a good grade, for example)
  • Gets a little grumpy at children for being disrespectful
  • Tries to include everyone as much as possible
